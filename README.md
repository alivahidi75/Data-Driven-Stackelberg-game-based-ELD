Data-Driven Incomplete Information Stackelberg Game Approach for Economic Load Dispatch in Power Grid.

The economic load dispatch (ELD) problem is a vital component of efficient power grid operation. While classical formulations assume generator cooperation, modern restructured power grids feature strategic, self-interested interactions, necessitating a hierarchical decision-making framework. Conventional Stackelberg game models effectively capture leader-follower dynamics but rely on unrealistic complete-information assumptions, under which the leader knows competitors' cost function coefficients. To bridge this gap, this paper proposes a novel data-driven Stackelberg framework for solving ELD under incomplete information. Instead of explicitly modeling followers' optimization, the leader utilizes an artificial neural network (ANN) trained on historical data to learn their best-response behaviors, specifically dispatch decisions and resulting locational marginal prices (LMPs). This approach transforms the bi-level optimization into a streamlined, single-level learning-based optimization. Furthermore, to address uncertainty regarding followers' cost parameters, a robust min-max strategy is integrated, enabling the leader to maximize profit even under worst-case scenarios. Simulation results on the IEEE 14-bus test system demonstrate that the proposed method accurately reproduces the model-based Stackelberg equilibrium and outperforms scenario-based Monte Carlo (MC) simulation in terms of cost estimation accuracy under uncertainty. The proposed framework offers a robust solution for strategic decision-making in competitive power grids where information transparency is limited.
